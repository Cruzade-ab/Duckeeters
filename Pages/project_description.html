Lego Serious Play

LEGO Serious Play is a facilitation methodology developed at The LEGO Group. It facilitates thinking, communication, and problem-solving techniques that draw on extensive research in fields such as psychology, business, and engineering. It's suitable for any individual who wants to explore complex ideas, improve team dynamics, or solve intricate problems. This methodology was incorporated with the Scrum methodology, to amplify creativity, enhance collaboration, and unleash innovation within teams. By combining the structured framework of Scrum with the playful and hands-on approach of LEGO Serious Play, organizations can build not only products but also a culture of continuous learning and adaptability.

Phase 1: Project Exploration

The phase exploration is about studying the deliverables, its requirements, and foreseeing the possible solutions to the problem. Here, the product backlog is constructed along with the first tasks to do in sprint one. The phase of exploration is crucial because of its role in setting the foundation for the entire project. During this phase, teams analyze requirements, anticipate challenges, and lay out the initial roadmap.

Phase 2: Manual Designs

The first sprint was focused on the meticulous design of the manuals. These innovative manuals had the sole purpose of illustrating step by step the construction of two unique and fun models of ducks, thus Quaky and Bricky were born! Each manual had its own distinctive style, combining clear instructions with playful illustrations to engage users in the delightful process of creating their blocky companions.

Phase 3: Video creations

The second sprint was focused on creating two interactive and fun videos, where exciting animations, colors and duckies come to life. These videos had the sole purpose of immersing users in the manual’s content, making the construction process engaging, dynamic and finally, providing feedback for the users’ good work. Each video has its charm, combining audiovisuals and a playful ambient to enhance the learning experience.

Phase 4: Project Testing

The third sprint was focused on testing the resources created, in this case the manuals and the videos. Each item was ensured to have clarity and accuracy, thanks to some users that participated actively in the testing, providing some valuable input that was considered and implemented in the next sprint. Their feedback reinforced the success of the project, and positive responses validated the effectiveness of the resources. Overall, the third sprint played a pivotal role in setting the foundation for the entire project, ensuring alignment with objectives and a well-prepared start.

Phase 5: Project Presentation

The fourth and final sprint was focused on presenting all the project made by ‘The Duckeeters’. It not only showcased the completion of the project, but also celebrated the collective creativity, innovation and effort put into the product. We, The Duckeeters, gladly present this website that compiles all the product, all in one place, with the sole purpose of inspiring future individuals with our work.
